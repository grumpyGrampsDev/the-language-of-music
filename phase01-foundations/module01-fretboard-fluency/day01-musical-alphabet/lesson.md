# Day 01 — The Musical Alphabet: Learning the Names

> Phase 1 • Foundations
>
> Module 1 • Fretboard Fluency

---

## Learning Objective

By the end of today's lesson you should:

- Know the seven musical letter names.
- Be able to recite the musical alphabet forward and backward.
- Understand that the letters repeat in a continuous cycle.
- Find the natural notes on the Low E string.
- Begin thinking in note names instead of fret numbers.

---

## Opening Thought

Before we can speak a language, we need to know its alphabet.

Music has one too.

A guitar can feel like a long row of numbered frets.

But frets are only locations.

Music happens in notes.

A guitarist might think:

> Third fret.

A musician thinks:

> G.

Today, we begin making that shift.

---

## The Musical Alphabet

The musical alphabet uses only seven letters:

```
A B C D E F G
```

After G, the alphabet begins again:

```
A B C D E F G A B C D E F G
```

Unlike the alphabet used for reading and writing, the musical alphabet does not end at Z.

It repeats forever.

```
A B C D E F G
A B C D E F G
A B C D E F G
```

These seven letters are the foundation for every note you will learn.

---

## Learning the Cycle

Say the musical alphabet aloud:

```
A B C D E F G
```

Then start again:

```
A B C D E F G A
```

Notice that A comes after G.

The letters do not stop.

They travel in a circle.

```
        A
      /   \
     G     B
    /       \
   F         C
    \       /
     E — D
```

You do not need to memorize that diagram.

Just remember:

> After G comes A.

---

## Starting from Different Notes

Musicians need to be able to begin the alphabet from any note.

For example, starting from C:

```
C D E F G A B
```

Starting from F:

```
F G A B C D E
```

Starting from B:

```
B C D E F G A
```

This may feel unfamiliar at first.

That is normal.

You are not learning a list.

You are learning a cycle.

---

## The Low E String

Now let’s place the musical alphabet on the guitar.

The thickest string is called the **Low E string** because its open note is E.

```
Open string = E
```

As you move up the string, the notes follow the musical alphabet.

For today, focus only on the natural letter names:

```
Open    E

1st     F

3rd     G

5th     A

7th     B

8th     C

10th    D

12th    E
```

At the 12th fret, we arrive at E again.

For now, simply notice that the letter names repeat.

You will explore why in the next lesson.

---

## The Fretboard Is a Map

Look at these examples:

```
3rd fret on the Low E string = G
```

```
5th fret on the Low E string = A
```

```
8th fret on the Low E string = C
```

The fret number tells you where your finger goes.

The note name tells you what sound you are making.

Your goal is to begin connecting both ideas:

```
Mind → Note → Location → Sound
```

For example:

```
G → 3rd fret on the Low E string → sound
```

Do not worry about instant recognition yet.

If you need to count through the alphabet, count.

Recognition comes through repetition.

## The Language of Music Principle #1

> **Music begins with names. Before you can speak the language, you must know its alphabet.**

---

## Key Takeaways

By the end of today you should know:

- The musical alphabet contains seven letter names: A, B, C, D, E, F, and G.
- After G, the musical alphabet begins again at A.
- The open Low E string is E.
- The natural notes on the Low E string are E, F, G, A, B, C, D, and E.
- Fret numbers tell you where to play; note names tell you what you are playing.
- Beginning to see notes instead of fret numbers is the first step toward fretboard fluency.
