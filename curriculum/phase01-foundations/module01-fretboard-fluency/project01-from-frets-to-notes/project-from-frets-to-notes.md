## Project Goal

During this project, you will revisit six discoveries:

1. Music has an alphabet.
2. That alphabet repeats.
3. Twelve notes exist before the pattern repeats.
4. Musical distance has consistent measurements.
5. Notes are identities, not locations.
6. The fretboard contains repeating relationships.

Today is not about learning something new.

It's about proving—to yourself—that you no longer see the guitar the way you did on Day 1.

This project is not a test of memory.

It is a demonstration of understanding.

# Part 4 — The Fretboard Tour

Now pick up your guitar.

Beginning on your chosen string:

- Play every note from the open string to the 12th fret.
- Say each note aloud before you play it.
- Listen to the pitch rising.
- Notice where there is no note between neighboring letters.
- Finish on the octave.

Now reverse the journey back to the open string.

If you make a mistake, don't simply correct it.

Ask yourself why your prediction was wrong.

Understanding grows much faster than memorization.

---

# Part 5 — Finding Familiar Faces

Choose any natural note.

Find it once.

Now ask yourself:

> **"Where else should this note exist?"**

Before touching the guitar, predict another location.

Then test your prediction.

Repeat with at least five different notes.

Each time you successfully predict another location, you're relying less on memory...

...and more on understanding.

# Definition of Done

Your Module 1 project is complete when you can honestly say:

- [ ] I understand the musical alphabet.
- [ ] I understand why the alphabet repeats.
- [ ] I understand why there are twelve unique notes.
- [ ] I understand half steps and whole steps.
- [ ] I understand what an octave is.
- [ ] I understand that a note is an identity rather than a location.
- [ ] I can predict where some notes repeat before I play them.
- [ ] I rely more on patterns than counting.
- [ ] I see the fretboard differently than I did when I started.
- [ ] I can ask questions about the fretboard instead of only searching for answers.

## Looking Ahead

When you began this module, the guitar probably looked like six strings stretched across a collection of numbered frets.

Now you've discovered something different.

Every fret.

Every string.

Every repeated note.

Every pattern.

They all belong to one connected musical system.

The fretboard is no longer a mystery.

It's becoming a language.

In Module 2, you'll begin learning its vocabulary.

Because once you can recognize the notes...

the next question is inevitable.

**What do those notes mean when they relate to one another?**
