# Changelog

All notable changes to **The Language of Music** will be documented in this file.

This project follows a milestone-based changelog rather than a strict software release cycle. Entries reflect meaningful progress in the development of the curriculum and its supporting resources.

---

## v0.2.0 — Building the Foundation

### Curriculum

- Completed **Module 1, Days 1–4**.
- Introduced the musical alphabet.
- Introduced octaves and repeating note identities.
- Introduced sharps, flats, and the chromatic system.
- Introduced half steps and whole steps as the foundation of musical distance.
- Established the recurring **Language of Music Principles** within each lesson.
- Refined the lesson structure to emphasize conceptual transformation over information delivery.

### Project Architecture

- Added **PHILOSOPHY.md** to define the educational philosophy of the project.
- Added **CURRICULUM_ROADMAP.md** to establish the long-term curriculum architecture.
- Added **CURRICULUM_GUIDE.md** to document lesson design, writing standards, and editorial principles.
- Completely redesigned the public **README.md** to communicate the project's vision and goals.

### Educational Direction

- Formally established the guiding philosophy:

  > **Teach understanding first, fluency second, and technique in service of expression.**

- Adopted conceptual transformation as the primary objective of every lesson.

- Defined the learner journey as a progression from mechanical thinking to musical understanding.

- Established curiosity as the driving force behind lesson sequencing.

## v0.1.0 — Project Begins

### Curriculum

- Began **The Language of Music**.
- Introduced the musical alphabet.
- Identified the natural notes on the Low E string.

### Project

- Created the initial repository.
- Established the long-term vision for an open-source music curriculum centered on understanding rather than memorization.
